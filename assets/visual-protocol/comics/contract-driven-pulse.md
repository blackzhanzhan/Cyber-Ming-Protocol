# Contract-Driven Pulse

## Core Judgment

Long-cycle development should not be governed by one scroll that grows fatter and fatter.
It should be governed by a sequence of pulse-sized Atomic Execution Contracts renewed by the human hub.

## Panels

### Panel 1

- Focus: a giant spec scroll grows heavier while Xiao Huanglong and Xu Jie struggle to hold it open
- Caption: "One giant blueprint becomes heavy to maintain."

### Panel 2

- Focus: workflow boxes keep moving, but Yan Song still pushes through them with momentum
- Caption: "Process order alone does not govern the cut being made now."

### Panel 3

- Focus: Xiao Huanglong stamps one small contract, routes it to Xu Jie, then hands it to Yan Song for the current slice
- Caption: "Pulse-sized contracts govern the live cut."

### Panel 4

- Focus: a chain of smaller contracts continues forward like linked steps instead of one giant scroll
- Caption: "Long cycles come from renewed contracts, not one swollen document."
